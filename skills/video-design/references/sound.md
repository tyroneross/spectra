# Sound (quick card)

Full prose + research citations: `SOURCE-GUIDE.md` Part IV.

## Music by audience
| Product / audience | Direction | BPM |
|---|---|---|
| AI agent / dev tool | Sparse, ambient, electronic, no lyrics | 80–100 |
| Consumer iOS | Warm, bright, melodic, upbeat | 100–120 |
| macOS / productivity | Clean, minimal, slight texture | 90–110 |
| Enterprise B2B | Confident, forward-moving, professional | 100–115 |
| Apple Watch / health | Light, optimistic, energetic | 110–125 |
| Product Hunt launch | Punchy, fast, builds | 115–130 |

## Mixing
- Music at **-15 to -20 dBFS** under VO — felt, not heard. Dip another 3–6dB during
  heavy on-screen text or UI focus moments.
- **VO at -6 to -3 dBFS.**
- Instrumental-only under VO — audible English lyrics compete directly with speech
  processing; never mix lyrics behind narration or subtitle-heavy content.

## Voiceover pace (WPM)
| Pace | WPM | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Slow/deliberate | 110–120 | E-learning, complex tech narration, non-technical enterprise |
| Conversational | 130–150 | Most explainers, tutorials, YouTube |
| Up-tempo | 150–170 | Feature launches, founder-led LinkedIn |
| Commercial/fast | 170–200 | Social ads, short-form, launch energy |
| >200 | — | Fatigue threshold — comprehension degrades |

**150-word rule:** 150 words ≈ 1 minute of natural VO. A 60s script over ~225 words
sounds rushed or forces frantic-feeling edits.

## Silence
Add 0.5–1s of relative quiet before and after the video's single most important claim
or metric — not dead air, it's processing time + emphasis. Also use to break tension
right before the CTA.

## Voice tone by audience
| Audience | Style | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Developers | Peer-level, dry, technical | Over-enthusiastic marketing voice |
| Enterprise | Confident, measured, authoritative | Startup-casual, overly upbeat |
| SMB/prosumer | Friendly, practical, warm | Corporate stiffness, jargon |
| Consumer | Energetic, natural, delightful | Robotic/monotone AI VO |
| Product Hunt/launch | Founder energy, genuine | Polished ad voice — reads as inauthentic |
