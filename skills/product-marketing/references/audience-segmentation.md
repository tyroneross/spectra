# Audience segmentation (quick card)

Same product, different video. Resolve the audience first — it changes everything.
Full prose + best practices: `SOURCE-GUIDE.md` Part II.

## Developer / technical (most skeptical; detects marketing-speak instantly)
- **Wants**: real code/terminal/errors; the "why" behind architecture; peer credibility
  (an engineer, not a marketer); time-to-value in <30s; depth over emotion.
- **Kills credibility**: pseudo-code/simplified demos, buzzwords ("future of work"),
  non-technical presenter, slow intros, generic music.
- **Formats**: tutorial/code-along (5–15m), quick-start (3–5m), architecture (5–10m),
  changelog (2–5m). **Channel**: YouTube primary; README/docs embed; Discord/HN.
- **Polish**: low–medium → Spectra `cool`, real IDE/CLI.

## Enterprise buyer / evaluator (evaluates risk, ROI, security, fit — not features)
- **Wants**: proof at scale, quantified outcomes, social proof from similar companies,
  clear implementation story, risk-reduction (support/SLA/compliance).
- **Formats**: ROI breakdown (2–5m), case study (3–8m), testimonial (2–4m),
  implementation walkthrough, objection-handling FAQ. **Channel**: sales decks, CRM, pricing page.
- **Polish**: medium–high → `bold`; bad audio is disqualifying.

## SMB / prosumer (buys on speed, simplicity, price-value; self-serve)
- **Wants**: fast clarity (what/cost/why-care), ease-of-use signals, relevant use cases.
- **Formats**: explainer (60–90s), feature spotlight (1–3m), relatable testimonial (60–90s).
- **Polish**: medium → `cool`/`warm`.

## Consumer (iOS/Watch — highest emotion, fastest hook; decides in seconds)
- **Wants**: "I get it" in 3s, strong aesthetic, emotional resonance, social proof (reviews/downloads).
- **Channel**: App Store preview primary; Reels/TikTok/Shorts secondary.
- **Polish**: medium (aesthetic matters) → `warm`; front-load the aha.
